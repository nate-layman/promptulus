# Task Transparency Assessment

### Does your task need transparency?

The key question: **Do you need to understand how the answer was reached, or just that it's correct?**

Think of it like modeling: Are you trying to understand something about how a system works (transparency matters), or just predict an outcome accurately (the result is what counts)?

---

## The Transparency Spectrum

| Level | Description | What It Means |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Transparency** | Someone needs to see and understand how the work was done. The method matters — for auditing, for learning, for trust, or for building on the work later. | Use transparent, explainable methods. Spreadsheets, documented procedures, reproducible analyses. AI shouldn't lead the core work. |
| **Low Transparency** | Only the accuracy and quality of the final product matters. Nobody asks how you got there. | AI can take the lead. Focus your energy on checking the result, not documenting the process. |

---

## Quick Self-Check

Ask yourself: **"If someone asked me to explain how I did this, would it matter?"**

- If an auditor, sponsor, reviewer, or supervisor would need to trace the steps → **High Transparency**
- If only the final product gets evaluated and nobody asks about the method → **Low Transparency**

When in doubt, start with more transparency. It's easier to loosen up later than to reconstruct a paper trail after the fact.
